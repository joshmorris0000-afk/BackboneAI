# Business Model

## What Backbone AI Sells

Backbone AI is a **managed operational intelligence service** for UK manufacturing and logistics businesses.

The product is not software. It is not consulting. It is an ongoing operational service that:

1. Connects a client's fragmented data sources into a unified, structured data layer
2. Applies AI to surface patterns, cost leaks, and inefficiencies invisible in the raw data
3. Automates the underlying workflows driving the waste
4. Runs all systems permanently — the client does not manage tools, only reviews results

---

## The Three Levels of Value

### Level 1 — Automation (entry point)
Replacing manual, repetitive tasks with AI-operated equivalents.

**Examples:**
- Invoice processing: AI extracts, matches POs, flags anomalies
- QC reporting: Auto-generated from raw production data
- Compliance docs: Drafted from operational records

**Client benefit:** Time saved, errors reduced, staff freed for higher-value work.
**Sales benefit:** Fast, provable, tangible ROI — typically within 6 weeks of go-live.

### Level 2 — Intelligence (the core differentiator)
Surfacing patterns that the client cannot currently see — because the data is fragmented.

**Examples:**
- "Supplier X has had a 34% late delivery rate increase over 90 days"
- "Your defect rate spikes every time Line 3 runs a batch change"
- "Based on order history, you'll have a stockout on SKU-441 in 19 days"

**Client benefit:** Better decisions, prevented problems, avoided costs.
**Business benefit:** This is what creates dependency — clients cannot get these insights anywhere else without Backbone AI's data layer.

### Level 3 — Transformation (long-term)
When AI is embedded in how strategic decisions are made — pricing, staffing, supplier contracts, capacity planning — the business operates fundamentally differently.

**Client benefit:** Competitive advantage, not just efficiency.
**Business benefit:** Highest contract value, lowest churn, strongest retention.

## Revenue Model

### Setup Fee (one-off)

Covers the cost of: data audit, data cleaning and structuring, system connections, AI build and testing, go-live support.

| Plan | Setup Fee |
|---|---|
| Pilot | £5,000 |
| Scale | £10,000 |
| Backbone | £15,000 |

This ensures clients are invested in the engagement and covers initial delivery costs.

### Monthly Retainer (recurring)

Covers the cost of: ongoing system operation, monitoring, iteration, performance reporting, account management.

| Plan | Monthly Retainer |
|---|---|
| Pilot | £1,500 |
| Scale | £3,500 |
| Backbone | £6,500 |

### Performance Share (aligned incentive)

A percentage of **measurable, documented savings** generated that month. Applied from Month 2 onwards (Month 1 is the establishment baseline).

| Plan | Performance Share |
|---|---|
| Pilot | None |
| Scale | 15% of documented monthly savings |
| Backbone | 20% of documented monthly savings |

**Example (Scale plan client, Month 3):**
- Invoice processing saves £2,100
- Route optimisation saves £3,800
- Compliance docs save £1,600
- Total documented savings: £7,500
- Performance share (15%): £1,125
- Total invoiced: £3,500 + £1,125 = £4,625
- Client net gain: £7,500 − £4,625 = **£2,875 profit**

This model makes the commercial case self-evident: the client only pays more when they earn more.

## Target Customer

### Primary (go-to-market)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sector | Manufacturing, Logistics & Distribution |
| Geography | United Kingdom |
| Size | 20–500 employees |
| Turnover | £2m – £50m |
| Tech maturity | Low to medium (ERP or spreadsheet-based, no existing AI) |
| Pain trigger | Admin overhead, operational inefficiency, compliance burden |

### Why this segment

- Only 5% of UK manufacturing firms use AI (ONS, 2025) — 95% of the market is unserved
- UK manufacturers lose £4bn/year to admin waste (YouGov/SafetyCulture)
- The sector is cash-conscious and ROI-focused — outcome-based pricing removes the risk barrier
- Strong word-of-mouth culture: win one logistics firm in a region and referrals spread quickly
- Use cases are highly consistent across firms (invoicing, compliance, route planning) — enabling repeatable playbooks

## Sales Process

### Stage 1 — Inbound / outreach
Client finds the website, submits the intake form, or is referred by an existing client.

### Stage 2 — Free operational audit (60 minutes)
- We review the intake form data and any uploaded documents beforehand
- Call structured around: current pain points, data sources, software stack, admin hours
- We identify 3–5 specific automation opportunities with estimated savings
- We present a tailored proposal (plan recommendation + projected monthly ROI)

### Stage 3 — Proposal and setup fee agreement
- Fixed scope: specific automations to build, data sources to connect, timeline
- Setup fee invoiced on agreement
- Access code issued, portal activated

### Stage 4 — Delivery (6 weeks)
- Week 1: Data audit
- Weeks 2–3: Data cleaning and structuring
- Weeks 3–5: AI system build
- Week 6: Live integration and go-live

### Stage 5 — Ongoing managed operations
- Monthly retainer invoiced on the 1st
- Performance share calculated and invoiced on the 15th (Month 2+)
- Monthly performance report shared via the client portal
- Quarterly business review (Scale and Backbone plans)

## Proprietary Assets Being Built

Every client onboarded contributes to Backbone AI's long-term competitive moat:

### 1. Sector benchmarking database
Anonymised, aggregated operational data from all clients. After 10 manufacturing clients, Backbone AI can tell a new client:
> "Manufacturers your size typically spend 22 admin hours per £1m revenue. You're spending 41. That gap costs you £87,000 a year."

No competitor without Backbone AI's client base can make this statement.

### 2. Repeatable playbooks
Each automation type (invoicing, routing, compliance) becomes more efficient to deploy with each client. The 10th invoice automation deployment takes a fraction of the time of the first.

### 3. Sector-specific AI model (Phase 2)
A fine-tuned language model trained on UK manufacturing and logistics data — compliance documents, quality standards, logistics terminology, HSE frameworks. A generic model knows everything generically. This model knows UK manufacturing specifically.
